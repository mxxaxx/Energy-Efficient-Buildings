Energy Efficiency

Construction Methods and why they work

Chimneys
One way of improving the energy efficiency of homes is to construct chimneys inside the houses.
This means that thermal energy can be transferred into the bedrooms upstairs instead of outside. This is useful energy.

Walls
The rate at which energy is transferred through walls of a house depends on four things:

  • The temperature difference between inside and outside.
  • The area of the walls. The larger the walls the harder they are to heat, as more thermal energy is required to heat by one degree Celsius.
  • The thermal conductivity of the walls. Some materials are conductors, others are insulators. The higher the thermal conductivity of a material, the higher the rate of energy transfer by conduction across the material.
  • the thickness of walls/windows is important. The thicker the walls, the slower the rate of energy loss.
Modern houses use two layers of brick to ensure they have thicker walls. This increases efficiency because the energy has to travel longer distances to get out of the house.
The house has cavity wall insulation between the layers of brick. The foam is trapped foal of air.
Because air is a good insulator, it helps keep the house warm by trapping the energy inside the house.

Loft insulation and carpets
One quarter of an uninsulated properties energy is lost through the roof. This is because of the convection process, which causes hot air to move up.
If the loft is insulated it stays warm. Carpets do the opposite they insulate from the floor. These aren't as important for the fact outlined before.
However can still be a valuable addition to the building. This is because an estimated 10% of energy is lost through uninsulated flooring. Carpets can
result in this being lessened. This benefit is doubled when there is a harsh winter.

The Physics Behind it all

Why houses are hot at the top

Image Source
The process above is called convection. Convection is simple once you understand it, but it can be a little peculiar to wrap your head around at first.
The principle is simple. Hot air goes up. Cool air goes down. The reasoning is a little more complicated
When air is hot the particles have more energy. They move around more. This means that they end up being more spread out and less dense.
In cold air, exactly the opposite occurs. The particles have less energy and clump together as if their trying to keep warm.
Because the cold air is more dense it sinks; the hot air is less dense so it rises. When the hot air reaches the top it loses it's energy
and sinks again. This is called convection.

The magical chain effect of conduction
Conduction is the transfer of energy through a solid. Note, it only works if the solid is a conductor.
In the side of the solid that has the highest temperature the particles are vibrating the most.
These particles bump into eachother. This chain reaction ends up making more particles vibrate and eventually the particles in the initially colder side are vibrating

Wind Turbines

Even though it sounds ridiculous, wind turbines are an effective way of increasing temperature within your homes. If you have a large garden
they can be extremely profitable. The Energy Saving Trust estimates that 250 pounds can be saved each year if a 6kW wind turbine pole is mounted in your garden
As wind turbines are renewable, they do not harm the environment very much.

Air Source heat pumps

If wind turbines are cool. Then I do not possess the vocabulary to explain how incredible air source heat pumps are.
It uses heat from the outside air to heat up your own home. Yes! This does mean that if you waste some energy

Triple Glazing

Triple glazed windows are extremely energy efficient. They work in the same way as double glazed windows but just with one extra layer.
They also come with the additional effect of reducing noise. However they are still very expensive and therefore not a realistic solution for most people.
